Why a Good Quotation Format Matters

A clear quotation helps customers understand costs, reduces confusion, and speeds up decisions. Whether you're a freelancer, shop owner, service provider, or agency, a neat quotation boosts trust and improves conversion rates.

What a Quotation Format Should Include

A reliable quotation format typically contains:

Business details

name, address, phone, email, GSTIN (if applicable)

Customer details

name, address, contact

Quotation number & date

Itemized list

item/service, description, quantity, unit price, total

Subtotal, taxes & grand total

GST/VAT breakdown

Terms & validity

payment terms, delivery lead time, expiry of quote

Signature line

or digital approval instructions

Tip: Keep layout simple and the totals easy to spot — buyers prefer clear numbers.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: What is a quotation format?

A quotation format is a structured document that lists products or services and their prices to provide a customer with an estimate.

Q2: Should I include GST/VAT in the quotation?

Yes — show taxes separately so customers can see the taxable amount and tax percentage.

Q3: Which file format is best to send?

Send a PDF for final quotes (non-editable). Keep an editable Word/Excel copy for internal edits.

Q4: How long should a quotation be valid?

Common practice is 7–30 days. Specify exact validity in the terms.

Q5: Can I convert a quotation into an invoice?

Yes. Once accepted, convert the quotation to an invoice and issue it for payment.
